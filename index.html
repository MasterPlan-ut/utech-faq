Admissions

+
How do I apply?
Apply online via the University's website and submit certified supporting documents (stamped/signed by a Minister of Religion, Justice of the Peace, Attorney-at-Law, or Medical Practitioner). Original documents should only be submitted upon request. Ensure all uploads are certified.

Supporting documents include:
  • Academic certificates
  • Birth certificate (Jamaican Birth Certificate or Jamaican Certificate of Citizenship)
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Tax Registration Number (TRN)
  • National ID (Passport, Driver's Licence or Voter's ID) if applicable
  • Transcripts / Progress Report:
    • Current UTech students/alumni applying for a new Undergraduate course: do not send a transcript; Admissions will attach your progress report.
    • Current UTech students/alumni applying for a new Postgraduate course: Admissions will request your transcript internally from the Student Records Office.
    • New prospective students (never attended UTech): submit official transcript(s) from previous tertiary institution(s) by the application deadline. (No secondary-school transcript is required.)
Forms not properly completed, without proof of processing fee (for manual submissions), or without required documentation will not be processed.

* Original documents should only be submitted upon request by the University. Documents tampered with will not be accepted, and the applicant will forfeit chances of admission to the University.

* Ensure that all supporting documents are certified (stamped and signed by a Justice of the Peace or Notary public) before uploading them with the application.

Is online my only option to apply?
If you are unable to apply online, you may print an application form from the University's website or collect one from the Office of Admissions and Enrolment Management (Mon–Fri, 8:00 am–4:00 pm).
How can I submit my supporting documents?
  1. Email as attachments to applicationdocuments@utech.edu.jm
  2. Registered mail or courier to: Admissions and Enrolment, University of Technology, Jamaica, 237 Old Hope Road, Kingston
  3. Secure online document portal
  4. Deliver in person (Papine campus or Western Campus, 17 Dome Street, Montego Bay)
What is the cost of the application?
Non-refundable fee: JA$1,000 (Jamaican residents) or US$40 (non-Jamaicans). Pay at Accounts before collecting a form or upon submitting a downloaded form.
My status shows "expired." How do I resume studies?
"Expired" means you did not meet award requirements within the maximum time. You may re-apply for readmission; any award conferred after completion is subject to the Transfer & Exemption Policy. Maximum permissible time is the prescribed duration + two years.
When does the application period start?
October 1 (or first weekday in October).
What are the application deadlines?
  • Early Bird: December 1 (or first weekday of the month)
  • Regular: March 31 (or last weekday of the month)
Apply early for priority consideration (especially oversubscribed programmes, summer, and international applicants).
When will I receive a decision?
  • Early Bird: by January 31
  • Regular: by June 30
  • Summer courses: by March 30
Queries: admissions@utech.edu.jm.
Do you accept late applications?
Yes, subject to space availability and you meeting qualifications, up to the start of the academic year. Normal procedures apply.
Can I apply to multiple courses of study?
You may select a second choice if space is available.
I was previously denied—should I reapply?
Yes. Applications apply only to that academic year; resubmit the required documents with a new application.
Is there an age requirement?
Applicants must meet matriculation requirements and be at least 17 years old by December of the year of admission.
What is the ECD?
A non-refundable Enrolment Commitment Deposit paid to confirm acceptance and applied to fees upon enrolment.
Entry requirements for a Bachelor's?
Minimum of five (5) CSEC or GCE O-Level subjects including Mathematics and English. See programme pages for specific requirements.
Can I defer my offer?
Yes. Apply for deferral via the Students Relations Management System; paying the ECD is part of approval.
How do I get advanced placement?
Holders of UCJ-accredited (or equivalent) Associate's Degrees/Diplomas may articulate with advanced standing.
Mature entry (special requirement)?
Applicants 25+ who don't meet normal requirements may apply for mature entry based on attainments/work experience. Prior learning is assessed under the University's PLA Policy.

Fee Payments / Registration / Account Balance

+
What is the Enrolment Commitment Deposit (ECD)?
The ECD is a mandatory, non-refundable deposit toward each semester's tuition. It is required to access module selection.
What are the ECD fees?
  • Local students: JMD $40,000
  • International students: USD $250
Why do we pay the ECD?
It signals a serious intention to enroll and represents ancillary charges applied toward overall fees.
How would I know my tuition fees?
Use the University's tuition calculator (link to be added).
What are the requirements to get registered?
  1. Pay your ECD (use approved payment agencies only).
  2. On the Student Portal: select & confirm modules (new students usually have a fixed diet).
  3. View fee breakdown for the semester.
  4. Choose a payment plan, accept fee terms and conditions and terms of studentship.
  5. Pay tuition based on the plan selected. Financial clearance updates in ~2 clear business days (Mon–Fri).
Are books included in tuition?
No.
Do I have to pay tuition in full at the start?
No. A payment schedule/plan is posted each semester on the portal; tuition depends on your registered modules.
Will I receive financial clearance without paying at least 50%?
No. You need ≥ 50% payment to receive clearance and complete registration/access services.
Will I be able to sit final exams after paying only 50%?
No. Full tuition must be paid to sit final exams.
What happens if I can't pay the remaining 50% before finals?
You will not be allowed to sit exams; access to timetables/services may be denied. Modules may be removed and 50% becomes due. If in difficulty, contact the Department of Student Financing: fincustomersupport@utech.edu.jm.
Do payments at NCB/Paymaster/UTech post immediately for clearance?
No. Allow 2 clear business days (Mon–Fri) for processing.
How do I query my account/balance?
Log in to the Student Portal → Student tab → Available Reports → Account Balance Report. For help email fincustomersupport@utech.edu.jm.
How do I request a fee breakdown letter for a financial institution?
Email feeestimate@utech.edu.jm. Processing typically takes a minimum of five (5) to seven (10) working days.
If my account is in arrears, can I sit final exams?
  • Self-financed / partially sponsored: All fees must be cleared to sit exams.
  • Fully sponsored: Allowed to sit if fully covered by sponsor/scholarship.
  • SLB loan: Allowed if fees do not exceed loan; otherwise pay the difference.
What are the late payment consequences?
Late payments attract a 2% fee on the remaining balance (calculated on the second half of tuition). Students must pay 50% to register and the second 50% in full to sit finals.
What account number do I use at payment agencies?
Agencies have the University's account numbers. Provide: Your Name, Student ID (this is the account number for NCB Bill Pay), Institution (University of Technology, Jamaica), and Payment Type (tuition/boarding).
How do I apply for an academic record document (for bank, etc.)?
Via the website → Online Academic Record Request System. Pay only through the agencies listed on the application page.
If an agency remits to UTech after the due date, will I be charged a late fee?
No, if your payment to the agency was on time. Late remittance is between the University and the agency.
I overpaid. How do I get a refund?
Submit the electronic Refund Application via the Student Portal → upload required documents and receipts to refundapplication@utech.edu.jm. Minimum processing time: 21 working days.
Can I register with an outstanding balance?
No. Settle all outstanding balances and pay the mandatory ECD to access module selection.
If I already have health insurance, do I still pay the fee on my breakdown?
Yes. No student is exempt; this provides an additional policy that can be used alongside existing coverage.
I paid per my fee breakdown—am I registered?
No. You are registered only after completing the 10-step enrolment process in the Student Portal.
If I am partially sponsored, can I make part-payments?
Follow the payment plan posted to your portal by the Department of Student Financing.
Can I register with a Commitment Letter?
Yes, once the letter is approved by a Finance Officer or the Financial Aid Office.
Can I pay my fees in USD if quoted in JMD?
Yes.

Can tuition/boarding be paid at the on-campus Cashier?
No. Pay via approved agencies: UTech Online Payment Portal, Paymaster, Bill Express, NCB Bill Pay, or JN Bank.
Can I make part-payment for accommodation?
Accommodation is quoted per semester; the University payment plan outlines required installments each semester.

SLB Applicants / Loans

+
If I have applied for a Students' Loan am I required to pay the ECD to access module selection?
Yes. All Students must pay the ECD to access module selection
How do I know if my SLB loan is approved?
Check your status on the SLB student portal.
Do I need to notify the University of my SLB status?
No. SLB communicates your loan status to the University.
What is the Loan status used by the University to update my loan to the system?
Loans with status "D" are updated on the University system.
Will I be granted financial clearance when my loan is updated on the system?
Yes, loan updated on the system will grant financial clearance.
How do I know the amount SLB will pay?
The approved loan amount is included in the students contract with the SLB, this amount is updated on the University system and can be viewed by students under Sponsors to my accounts on the students' portal.
Will the University communicate with the SLB to provide my registration status?
Yes, the University will communicate your registration status to the University.
How will SLB remit my tuition payment to the University?
The SLB will make payment of your tuition based on your semester invoice, payments are made after students are registered and their registration status communicated to the SLB.
If I pay my fees to register and then I am approved for a SLB loan during the year, when will I receive a refund?
A student can only be refunded his/her portion after SLB remits payment to the University.
How will UTech, Ja. treat excess funds received from the Students' Loan Bureau?
The Students' Loan Bureau is committed to paying Tuition and Exam fees only. All excess funds received must be returned to the Bureau by the University.
What will happen if the Students' Loan Bureau remits my loan short?
The outstanding balance will become the student's responsibility to pay.
If my loan is approved by the Students' Loan Bureau and I have completed all the necessary processes at the Bureau, will UTech,Ja. ensure that the Bureau remits payment on my behalf?
No. It is the sole responsibility of the student to ensure that the Students' Loan Bureau remits payment to the University. Students can check with the Students Loan Bureau for disbursement information.
How will I know the actual payment that the Students' Loan Bureau remits to the University?
All students whose fees are paid by the Students' Loan Bureau (SLB) must visit the student portal to view payment from their Sponsor.
If the Students' Loan Bureau (SLB) pays my fees, will I also be awarded the 'Grant-In-Aid'?
The Students' Loan Bureau awards 'Grant-In-Aid' to students after evaluating their applications. However, students should check with SLB, to see if they were approved for a grant-in-aid.
What if I only need the 'Grant-In-Aid' and not the loan?
The Grant-In-Aid is approved for students deemed needy by the Students' Loan Bureau and, as such, students who do not need the Loan will not be given the Grant-In-Aid.
What will happen if I receive a full scholarship after being approved for the Students' Loan and Grant-In-Aid?
The Students must complete and sign a declaration form addressed to the SLB, this will indicate whether the student keep both the loan and the scholarship or return the loan. This form must be submitted through the Scholarship Office who will prepare and submit the request to the Bureau on behalf of the students.
If I receive an exemption for a certain number of modules will there be a reduction in my tuition fees?
No. Students are not entitled to a reduction in their fees if they have received exemptions.
How do you pay to "top-up" your printing quota at SCIT, CSA, and SBLM for print credit?
Pay at the Accounts Department, present your ID and state the number of pages you intend to buy. Proceed to the respective lab with your receipt.
If my Identification card is lost, what should I do to replace it?
Pay for a reprint at the Accounts Department and proceed to the Admissions Office for a memo to be prepared. Submit this memo to the Safety and Security Department for a reprint of the card.
Can tuition and boarding fees be paid at the Cashier Unit on campus at UTech, Ja.?
No, Tuition and Boarding payments are not accepted at the Cashier's Unit on campus. All tuition and or boarding fees must be paid at the approved agencies: UTECH online Payment Portal, Paymaster, Bill Express, NCB Bill payment option or JN Bank.
Can we make part payment for accommodation?
Accommodation fee breakdown is quoted per semester; the University fee payment plan will provide details of payment requirements per semester.
Can I get an official statement of my account balance?
Students are required to request a letter from the Department of Student Financing at feeestimate@utech.edu.jm. This process usually takes at least seven (7) working days. Students can also visit the Student Portal to see their transactions history.
What are the conditions to be placed on academic probation?

A student with an accumulative GPA lower than 2.0 at the end of a semester must seek academic advisement. If the GPA at the end of the academic year is below 2.0 the student will be placed on academic probation and must remain in the level/year last registered until a GPA of 2.0 or above is achieved. The maximum number of credits, including the failed modules, which can be attempted in any semester or summer session, is 12 and 9, respectively. Students on academic probation are required to meet regularly with their assigned Academic Advisor to:

  • Discuss reasons for unsatisfactory academic performance;
  • Develop strategies to achieve satisfactory academic standing and monitor progress towards that goal.

A student failing to maintain a minimum accumulative GPA of 2.0 and above over two (2) consecutive years of enrolment will be discontinued from their course of study.

Earn And Study

+
Are students allowed to work on campus during the semester?

All registered students are eligible for on campus employment. Employment is provided for students across campuses (Papine, Montego Bay, Slipe Road, Authur Wint Drive) through the Earn and Study Programme.

Work is scheduled to avoid conflict with the students' academic performance. Students who wish to participate in the Earn and Study Programme may choose one of the following options:

Option 1 – Working in Semesters 1 & 2 (50 hrs/fortnight for both semesters 1& 2) part-time

Option 2 – Working in the summer session (40 hrs/week for 14 weeks) fulltime

Please note the following:

  • First (1st) year students are NOT allowed to work in Semesters 1 & 2 but can work in the summer session of their first (1st) year.
  • Final year students are NOT allowed to work in Semesters 1 & 2 but can work in the summer session of their final year.

Application forms are made available online before the start of each Programme, and the application periods for "Earn and Study" are as follows:

  • Semesters I & II – August.
  • Summer Session – March.

Earn and Study currently pays $350.00/hr

What documents are required when applying for Earn and Study?
  • A valid Student ID
  • Your progress report
  • An NCB MasterCard (via UTech)

NB: Payments are made to the NCB MasterCard only. Students are required to apply for an NCB MasterCard when submitting their application for the Earn and Study Programme. NCB MasterCard application forms are available at the Student Services front desk. If you already have an NCB MasterCard through UTech, having applied for one for the Meal Programme or from working on the Earn & Study Programme, there is no need to apply for a new one.

Exemption

+
What is an exemption?

It is (a) the award of credits for workplace or professionally certified experience where the learning outcomes are deemed equivalent to those prescribed in a UTech, Ja. module, and/or (b) the award of credits for equivalent module successfully pursued at an approved institution, which were not considered during the initial application to the course of study.

What is the credit limit and acceptability for exemptions?
  • Students are expected to complete at least fifty percent (50%) of the course of study as registered students of the university, including 60% of levels 3 and 4 modules to receive a university award.
  • A student will only be allowed one exemption from a cooperative Education module in any course of study.
  • To be eligible for exemption, a module must have been completed within five years prior to the year of application for module exemption, in a UTech course of study or at another approved/accredited tertiary institution.
  • The related work experience in industry must be acquired for a period as specified by the respective school.
What is the procedure for applying for an exemption?
  • Complete the Application for Module Exemption form, which is available in the Faculty/College Student Affairs Office. Exemption for service modules must be done on a separate form.
  • Submit the completed form(s) to the Faculty/College Student Affairs Office along with all supporting documentation (including module description, transcript, syllabus, and statement of achievement and level achieved) no later than two weeks prior to the start of the semester in which the exemption is being sought. If the request for exemption is based on related work experience in industry, also submit verification letter with job description attached. Further, if the student is the proprietor of the business in which the experience is gained, submit supporting documentation that has been notarized.
  • The appropriate Subject leader/Programme Director/Module Coordinator will evaluate the application (for comparability of the quality/suitability of the experience/module). For work-related experience, the Programme takes a Challenge Examination or attend an interview before arriving at a final decision.

The Faculty/College Student Affairs Office will inform the student in writing the result of the evaluation by the end of the fourth week of classes. If exemption is granted, the student is not required to complete the Add/Drop form unless he/she wishes to add other module(s).

How do I effect a transfer?

A transfer takes place when there is a change from one campus or course specialization to another. Application should be submitted to the applicant's Programme Director six (6) weeks before the beginning of the Semester using the "Request for transfer" form.

  • (i) Complete the "Request for Transfer form", in Triplicate. The form is available in your College/Faculty/School office and the University's website.
  • (ii) Submit to your current Head of School before July 31.

Note: Transfers are not automatic and will be influenced by a variety of factors including your qualifications, academic status, aptitude for the discipline and space availability.

How do I apply for an academic record (transcript)?

A transcript is an academic record of the programme and grades for modules (passed/failed) taken by a student and any certification earned.

(i) Visit the website and select Online Academic Record Request System

(ii) Pay the fee, (Transcript, JA$1,000, Status letter/Statement, JA$1000), at Accounts Receivables.

Please note: If your ID number is alphanumeric (eg. S142) you are only able to pay at the Accounts Department. For strictly numeric Id numbers, you may pay at the Paymaster or Bill Express agency.

How do I apply for a change of name?

Complete a Change of Information form available at the front desk of the Student Records Office is located within the Administration Building, at any time during your tenure.

Important Note: A name change cannot be effected after a student has left the University.

How do I replace my ID card?

(i) Pay the $1,000 fee at Accounts Receivable

(ii) Take the receipt to the Admissions Office, which will make arrangements with the Safety & Security Department for a replacement.

How do I correct information on an ID card?

Report the matter to the Admissions Office, which will make arrangements with the Safety & Security Department for a replacement card to be done.

How do I prepare for graduation?

(i) Register on the graduation link and follow the instructions on the website.

(ii) Make payment according to the respective service that you require.

How many modules do I need to complete in order to graduate?

Modules are indicated as being at Levels 1–4, based on the difficulty/complexity of the modules and where they are sequenced in the Course of Study. Each student must complete the required number of credits at every Level in order to graduate. Please consult with your College or Faculty for further information.

Am I allowed to complete my course of study before the prescribed timeframe?

The completion of a course of study in a shorter timeframe based on a students' decision to increase his/her credit load in a given academic year as approved by the Academic Advisor or Programme Director.

What is academic advisement?

Academic advisement is a structured support system available to every student when making important academic decisions related to his/her course, available options, electives, and academic progress in general. Academic advisement is managed in each Faculty or College, by the Faculty or College Academic Advisement Unit (FAAU/CAAU).

What is the faculty / college academic advisement unit (FAAU /CAAU)?

The FAAU/CAAU serves as the hub of the advisement system in each College or Faculty and monitors the academic advisement system to assist students on academic probation restructure their programme diets. Consultations are held with students who were referred to the FAAU/CAAU by their Academic Advisor for more intensive academic advisement.

What happens if a student is underperforming?

A student must maintain a satisfactory academic standing to progress from year to year and level to level in a given Course of Study. Students who fail to make satisfactory progress will be placed on academic probation.
